1. Introduction and Test Description üí°
This document provides a comprehensive analysis and explanation of the output generated from the 01_data_generation.py script, which simulates data for an A/B test on a YouTube platform, likely comparing two different video thumbnails (or similar features) to determine which one is more effective at driving user engagement (clicks).

A/B Testing is a statistical method used to compare two versions of a single variable (A and B) to determine which one performs better. In this context:

Version A (Control Group): Users see the existing or standard thumbnail/feature.

Version B (Treatment Group): Users see the new, experimental thumbnail/feature.

The goal is to measure a key metric, in this case, the click rate, and use statistical inference to conclude whether the difference in performance is due to the change (the new thumbnail) or simply random chance.

Based on the descriptive statistics provided:

The Treatment Group (new thumbnail) achieved a higher click rate (15.86%) compared to the Control Group (old thumbnail, 12.47%).

The observed lift in click rate is significant (3.39 percentage points).

Next Step for Full Analysis: To draw a statistically sound conclusion, one must perform an Inferential Statistical Test (e.g., a Z-test for two proportions or a chi-squared test). This test would calculate a p-value to determine the probability that the observed 3.39% difference is merely due to chance. If the p-value is below a significance level (e.g., 0.05), the result is deemed statistically significant, and we can confidently conclude that the new thumbnail is better. Since the data was synthetically generated with a higher click probability for the treatment group, a proper statistical test on this dataset is expected to show a statistically significant lift.
